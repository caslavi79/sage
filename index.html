About Liz

She starts by listening

Liz Childress is a licensed massage therapist who founded Sage Therapeutics with a simple belief: the conversation matters as much as the work. Every session begins with one.

Her training is in therapeutic massage, cupping, gua sha, and aromatherapy, but what people notice is the patience. She won't dig straight into the spot that hurts. If your lower back is locked up, she'll start by softening the muscles around it first, your hips, your sides, everything connected, so that by the time the work reaches the source, your body is actually ready to let it go. It's a slower approach. It's also why people leave feeling different than they expected.

The Practice

The tools we reach for

Cupping Therapy

Glass cups draw the fascia upward, bringing fresh blood to tissue that's been holding on too long. It looks dramatic. Most people find it deeply calming.

Gua Sha

A smooth stone worked along the skin to break up stagnation and bring circulation back to areas that have gone quiet. Old medicine, still effective.

Hot Stone Therapy

Heated basalt stones placed along the body to soften tight muscles before the hands ever get there. The warmth does half the work on its own.
